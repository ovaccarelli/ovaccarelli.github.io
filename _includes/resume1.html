Luxembourg

Computational and fundamental study of molecules and materials.

  • The structure and stability of molecular systems bonded by van der Waals (vdW) interactions are governed by the interplay between dispersion attraction and Pauli repulsion. Arising due to different physical origins, these forces do not seem to have a simple connection. The main goal of our work is to build a self-consistent model for studying these two energies based on the quantum drude model oscillator (QDO). Therefore, our findings hint to explore the potential usefulness of the QDO for vdW-bonded systems as a complete building block to develop next-generation quantum force fields.
  • Organic monolayers and thin films interacting with extended metal surfaces have been attracting much interest to develop new electronic devices. However, a correct description of the equilibrium structure and dynamic properties of these complex systems from first principles represent a challenging task to the state-of-the-art theoretical methods. In particular, the standard DFT methods underestimate the effect of the non-local dispersion interactions which play a dominant role in determining the structure and stability of organic molecules on solid surfaces. In this work, we use the recently developed first-principle approach, that properly takes into account van der Waals interactions dispersion effects for these organic-inorganic materials, to investigate the adsorption of acenes molecules on metal surfaces.

Holder of a Research Grant

Bari, Italy
2014 - 2015 | Physics Department “M. Merlin” - University of Bari

Ghost Imaging and Plenoptic Correlation.

  • In this project, I have studied Plenoptic imaging with thermal-light correlations. A camera is an optical device which creates a single image of an object. Ordinary cameras collect the light scattered from this object on a matrix of sensors (the CCD), but do not record the information about the direction of the light. The goal of the Plenoptic Camera is to re-capture this lost information, but, to achieve this, spatial resolution decreases. The thermal ghost-imaging technique is based on space-time coincidence counting and this correlation measurement is used to decoupling the detections of spatial and directional information in a Plenoptic Camera. In this study, I focused on theoretical aspects with the aid of simulations in Wolfram Mathematica.

Thesis:"Exotic phenomena in the new frustrated spin ladder Li2O(CuSO4)2"

  • During my PhD project, I have investigated the magnetic properties of a new frustrated two-leg spin ladder, Li2O(CuSO4)2, through a combined experimental and theoretical approaches. I acquired working on first-principle calculations (DFT and DFT+U), as they are currently implemented in QUANTUM ESPRESSO, and I solved spin Hamiltonian, parametrized ab-initio, using Exact Diagonalization. Furthermore, I also took a closer look in the world of experimental physics. In particular, I took part of IR spectroscopy measurements (at the IMPMC in Paris) and inelastic neutron scattering experiment (at the ILL in Grenoble) in order to investigate the magnetic excitations of our system in its low temperature dimerized phase.

Thesis:"Entanglement in many-body system"

  • In my Master thesis I have studied the correlation between the components of a spin-1/2 Heisenberg chain interacting with an external magnetic field.
